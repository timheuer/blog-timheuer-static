<p>i'm a food nut.  i pride myself on my ability to eat everything in sight.  seriously, i will.  i'll at least try something once.  there hasn't been anything presented in front of me that i haven't tried.  i'd probably draw the line at coagulated blood pizza or anything else presented on fear factor.</p>
<p>one of the only reasons i have cable television is for the <a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com">food network</a>.  when i first got married my new wife (she's still my wife, btw so i'm not sure why i'm classifying here here as new) was annoyed at my infatuation by a (then unknown) chef named emeril.  it was pretty much the only channel i watched.  i like to think that my nielsen ratings usage helped propel the food network into stardom...hey, i can dream can't i?  i watched all the food celeb up-and-comers, but one reigns supreme above all others (a close tie between him and bobby flay) and that is <a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/food/alton_brown/0,1974,FOOD_9782,00.html">alton brown</a>.  if you ever want to know anything about food, you should immediately set your tivo's to '<a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/food/show_ea/0,1976,FOOD_9956,00.html">good eats</a>' right now.  the man either has incredible writers/researchers or is immensely knowledgeable...and i vote for both.  regardless, the show is entertaining (as much as food shows go, i mean there are no special effects that blow up cars or anything) and informative.</p>
<p>alton also started a new show last year that has a 'season 2' this year entitled '<a href="http://www.foodnetwork.com/food/show_ab/0,2857,FOOD_26776,00.html">feasting on asphalt</a>' and is him and a bunch of other motorcyclists traveling across areas of the united states in search for unique food stops.  what i like about this show is it is a little less scripted, a little more raw, but still core to the main character: food.  it takes me along a journey with them (did i just say journey?) and i like it.</p>
<p>but as i started to watch this second season, i began to realize that i wanted more from the shows.  i wanted more about the areas that ended up on the cutting room floor.  but most of all, as a geek i saw too much potential go to waste about what they were doing.  how are the cameras hooked up on the motorcycle?  how are they getting good audio from the cyclists from the handhelds as they are driving?  where is the exact route on gps?  etc.  it made me think more about what that show (and network) could do.  here's my ideas.</p>
<p>first the show...</p>
<ul>
    <ul>
        <li>give me a blog/podcast/behind the scenes!  heck, i'll come aboard.  my love for technology and food, c'mon this is the perfect trip.  i want to hear about the cutting room floor stuff, the anecdotes of each stop, etc. -- hook me up some vidcasts alton! </li>
        <li>gps -- route yourself online -- you have a microsite for the show, give me some virtual earth love with points/stops/etc. -- then you can link them to the actual locations of the places you stop and/or vid/podcasts </li>
        <li>tech gear -- you are riding motorcycles...people like those...how about a little about the rides, the rigs, and the overall equip -- give us the behind-the-stage ticket! </li>
    </ul>
</ul>
