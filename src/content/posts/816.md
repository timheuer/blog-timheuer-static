<P>okay, so what happens when you get the call that a netadmin changed the local security policy and they can't get in anymore.</P>
<P>no problem, log into another machine and terminal service in, right?</P>
<P>&#8220;interactive logon denied by local policy&#8221; -- uh oh.</P>
<P>okay, manage the machine (not part of a domain) remotely and add a new user that is in a different group and grant remote login rights.</P>
<P>&#8220;logon denied&#8221; -- hmmm...uh oh.</P>
<P>me: &#8220;did you change the policy to Everyone?&#8221;<BR>netadmin: &#8220;i think so&#8221;<BR>me: uh-oh.</P>
<P>thanks to a little googling and the resource kit i was able to find something i wasn't aware of before...the ntrights.exe tool...to change rights remotely.</P>
<P>guess what, it worked and saved this server BIG TIME.</P>
<P>anyhow, if you didn't know about it...it is something handy to have around.</P>
