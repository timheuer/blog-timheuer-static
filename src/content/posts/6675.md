<p>to whomever services the united states postal service (usps) tracking applications, please remove them.&nbsp; they serve no purpose.&nbsp; or at least please rename them.</p>
<p>i <a href="http://timheuer.com/blog/archive/2004/06/08/684.aspx">ranted about how much usps tracking sucks</a>&nbsp;a while back but felt like ranting again.&nbsp; i bought something on ebay last week and the ebayer shipped it usps priority mail/trackable the next day (12/21).&nbsp; here is the &ldquo;tracking status&rdquo; as of today:</p>
<p>&ldquo;The U.S. Postal Service was electronically notified by the shipper or shipping partner on December 21, 2005 to expect your package for mailing.&nbsp; Information, if available, is updated every evening.&nbsp; Please check again later.&rdquo;</p>
<p>okay, a few things here:</p>
<p>1) &ldquo;<strong>information, if available</strong>, is updated every evening&rdquo; &mdash; if available?&nbsp; isn&rsquo;t that the point of tracking codes?&nbsp; if you don&rsquo;t have the information available, then what exactly are you tracking?</p>
<p>2) &ldquo;information, if available, is <strong>updated every evening</strong>&rdquo; &mdash; every evening?&nbsp; so much for realtime tracking.&nbsp; kinda defeats the purpose if a) i have to wait every night to find out where my package went that day and b) what time exactly is &ldquo;evening&rdquo; again?&nbsp; what&rsquo;s even funnier about this is if you view more information on the usps web site, you will see this:</p>
<p>&ldquo;..provides [information] stating the current delivery status information&rdquo;</p>
<p>interesting, i guess &ldquo;every evening&rdquo; = &ldquo;current delivery status&rdquo;</p>
<p>3) priority/express mail &ndash; from the usps web site you&rsquo;ll see that usps priority mail is 2&ndash;3 business days.&nbsp; 12/21 was a wednesday.&nbsp; i don&rsquo;t have my package yet.&nbsp; for those keeping score at home 4 days &gt; 2&ndash;3 days.&nbsp; when you ask usps? &ldquo;we don&rsquo;t guarantee the timeframe&rdquo; &mdash; sweet, but you&rsquo;ll charge me the premium with going to sleep at night no problem.&nbsp; &ldquo;especially around the holidays&rdquo; &mdash; okay, understandable, but again, don&rsquo;t inform customers that it is typical priority mail&hellip;call it semi-priority mail &mdash; or discontinue the service during the holiday months&hellip;fact being: if it isn&rsquo;t a priority piece of mail even over the standard holiday 1st class mail pieces, then don&rsquo;t charge for it &mdash; either treat it as such, or stop the service.</p>
<p>usps tracking sucks.&nbsp; i said it again.&nbsp; it isn&rsquo;t tracking, it isn&rsquo;t real-time, there isn&rsquo;t any mechanism for usps to even help you&hellip;it just isn&rsquo;t a value-add to the consumer.&nbsp; they need to drop the tracking portion and just call it the delivery confirmation&hellip;that&rsquo;s really what it is.&nbsp; they can tell you (hopefully) *when* it got delivered, but not anything up until that point.</p>
<p>usps, wake up.&nbsp; you aren&rsquo;t ups, fedex, dhl, or airborne.&nbsp; those companies know how to do these things.&nbsp; stick to mail and your offerings, but make sure you stick to what you are selling.&nbsp; if you aren&rsquo;t equipped for tracking and priority services, then don&rsquo;t do them.&nbsp; usps is going by the wayside anyway.&nbsp; stamp prices keep going up.&nbsp; the last time i used a stamp was this week &mdash; to send xmas cards &mdash; and that&rsquo;s only because i don&rsquo;t have emails for all my extended holiday card list o&rsquo; people.&nbsp; email is the typical communication channel for standard &ldquo;mail&rdquo; these days.&nbsp; i&rsquo;ve only used priority mail for things that i really don&rsquo;t need to get there tomorrow, but faster than normal (i&rsquo;ll give usps credit for being a low priced leader for their semi-priority mail service), but for everything else, i know i can cound on <a href="http://www.ups.com/">brown</a> or the <a href="http://www.fedex.com/">other guys</a> for sure.</p>
<p>UPDATE: as i post this now, usps just came to my door to deliver my package &mdash; maybe i should email them and let them know the status so i can retrieve it from the tracking site.&nbsp; it will be interesting to see how long the status takes to update&hellip;i signed up for the email tracking &ldquo;for all future activity&rdquo; &mdash; now that it is delivered, we&rsquo;ll see what that means.</p>
