<p>i&rsquo;m in san francisco for the <a href="http://msdn.microsoft.com/launch2005">microsoft launch activities </a>and while waiting to check in to my hotel i thought i&rsquo;d just wander around.</p>
<p>getting off the plane i decided to take the <a href="http://www.bart.gov/">bart</a> system to downtown rather than the typical taxi (and now that i have, not sure why i ever took a taxi).&nbsp; i wish i lived in a city with major public transportation all over the place.&nbsp; the ride was $4.95 (versus a $40 taxi) and got me there in about 10 minutes.&nbsp; </p>
<p>getting off the bart i noticed the station (powell station) was flooded with ipod advertisements all over the place &mdash; like every sign was <a href="http://www.apple.com/ipod">ipod</a>, the column pillars were ipod, the benches, everything.&nbsp; you&rsquo;d think there was an ipod conference.&nbsp; as i climbed the stairs i realized potentially one of the reasons why&hellip;at the top of the stairs of the powell station is an <a href="http://www.apple.com/retail/sanfrancisco/map/">ipod store</a>.&nbsp; even high above union square though &mdash; ipod nano all over the place.</p>
<p>i guess this struck me as in all other major conferences i&rsquo;ve gone to of microsoft&rsquo;s, the city is usually plastered with marketing of that conference.&nbsp; granted launch isn&rsquo;t as large (attendee speaking of course) as others, but why not put out the word?</p>
<p>sfo is a great place and if i could convince my family to move, it would be one i&rsquo;d consider &mdash; beautiful and cool (weather speaking) &mdash; remember i&rsquo;m from phoenix.&nbsp; i wandered all over downtown aimlessly just observing.&nbsp; i noticed a homeless man pressing the coin return button on a newspaper stand violently/consistently about 20 times in a row&hellip;then he&rsquo;d check the change bin&hellip;repeat 5 times&hellip;maybe he knows something we don&rsquo;t? i thought.</p>
<p>i also saw a bunch of photographers taking pictures of trolleys and people &mdash; something i would imagine isn&rsquo;t uncommon in other large metro cities.&nbsp; i really enjoyed all the social gathering and just business of the city&hellip;it was great to see that.&nbsp; there were some other weird things i saw (like a toilet/public restroom&nbsp;in the middle of the sidewalk that i must have walked past several times on previous viits), but in general, if you like people watching, sfo is a great place to be.</p>
<p>well, i&rsquo;m off to some pre-launch stuff and can&rsquo;t wait to see steveb tomorrow.</p>
