<P>if it is one thing that i really don&#8217;t like in the consumer electronics/computing industry, it is hearing about launches and product releases and not being able to immediately acquire them.</P>
<P><A href="http://www.microsoft.com/">present company included</A>, i think companies need to do a better job at getting &#8220;launch&#8221; products immediately in the hand of consumers.</P>
<P>yesterday, <A href="http://www.apple.com/">apple</A> announced several new products, one namely that may shakeup hardware vendors, namely the <A href="http://www.apple.com/macbookpro/">macbook pro</A>.&nbsp; now, it must be said that apple rules the market in hardware design for me.&nbsp; i think their innovation in computing hardware is solid, smart, sexy, and all the other buzzwords that people like.&nbsp; i have an <A href="http://www.apple.com/imac">iMac</A>, and everytime i look at it i bang my head at why the pc manufactures have not come up with a similar all-in-one *smart* design (yes, i&nbsp;know emachines tried, but in the same form factor that made the first imacs fumble a bit).&nbsp; my imac has one cord.&nbsp; one cord.&nbsp; say again &#8211; one cord.&nbsp; no messy cables, no huge boxes, just one cord connecting power to a beautiful display and piece of hardware.&nbsp; don&#8217;t even get me started on their laptops &#8212; sweet to say the least&#8230;beautiful pieces of hardware.</P>
<P>note: i&#8217;ve been commenting on their hardware only.&nbsp; i LOVE windows.&nbsp; i think for me, it is the best platform.&nbsp; for my mother-in-law factor, the jury is still out.&nbsp; i think windows rules on several fronts, none of which i&#8217;ll argue here, for that isn&#8217;t my point.</P>
<P>there have been news about how long it will take before windows (and vista) show up on a macbook pro.&nbsp; i would love to see that&#8230;however, i think it will be a challenge.&nbsp; one question i have lies in how difficult apple has made the hardware choices to find/hack/whatever any device drivers to work with the windows operating system.&nbsp; if someone can do it, then i&#8217;ll buying a macbook pro, guaranteed.&nbsp; </P>
<P>the second announcement they made was around their software offerings.&nbsp; <A href="http://www.ilife.com/">iLife 06</A> was announced.&nbsp; now having an imac, i already have iLife 05 and i think it is a great bundle for the home user (and arguably for the professional as well).&nbsp; namely the reason i have a mac is for video editing.&nbsp; there is no comparison between imovie and windows movie maker.&nbsp; imovie rocks. no question.&nbsp; iLife 06 brings enhancements in iPhoto and iMovie as well as introducing iWeb &#8212; a better software experience for lay users for blogging, etc.&nbsp; that was a smart move and helps drive revenue to their .mac offering.</P>
<P>now with both of these announcements i wanted to see them (and buy iLife 06).&nbsp; iWent to the apple store buy iCouldn&#8217;t find any of the announced products iLooked for!&nbsp; the apple store had nothing, and didn&#8217;t know when they&#8217;d get them.&nbsp; ARGH! it appears the hardware isn&#8217;t shipping until feb, but the software wasn&#8217;t their either.&nbsp; man iHate when companies do that! (again, present company included).&nbsp; don&#8217;t announce the availability &#8212; until it&#8217;s available!!!</P>
<P>i could go on with my thoughts of how apple is charging for incremental upgrades and not offering to exisitng same-OS users (see: frontrow, and photobooth), but i won&#8217;t.&nbsp; apple is a darling in a lot of people&#8217;s eyes namely because they hate microsoft.&nbsp; but in reality, they are no different.&nbsp; there is a cost to their OS (and by the way, they only support their OS on their hardware &#8212; clearly punctuated again by Jobs at the announcement yesterday), and they bundle their software, and they charge for updates.</P>
<P>well, iBetter get back to work&#8230;</P>
