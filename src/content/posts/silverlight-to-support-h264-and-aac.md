<p>So you want H.264 support in <a href="http://silverlight.net">Silverlight</a>?  So do we.  In fact we will be <strong>showing a preview of H.264 and AAC support in Silverlight </strong>at the International Broadcasting Conference (IBC) this week in Amsterdam.  Scott Guthrie comments on this saying:</p>
<blockquote>
<p><em>“</em><em>At IBC 2008 we will be demonstrating a technology preview of H.264 video and Advanced Audio Coding (AAC) audio playback support in Silverlight, and H.264 authoring using Microsoft Expression Encoder and Windows Server 2008 for delivery. Until now, Silverlight has supported the SMPTE VC-1 and Windows Media formats, as well as MP3 for audio, enabling customers to take advantage of broad support across the Windows Media ecosystem, including third-party tools, service providers and content delivery networks.</em></p>
<p><em>We’ve always wanted Silverlight to support a variety of formats, so today we’re announcing that H.264 and AAC support will be available in a future version of Silverlight, which will offer content owners greater flexibility and choice to deliver video and audio.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>One thing that I didn’t know was that Microsoft has been an active member in the standardization of H.264.  Microsoft’s Gary Sullivan was the chairman of the Joint Video Team (JVT) which developed the H.264 standard.</p>
<p>This is exciting news for the <span style="font-weight: bold;">future </span>of Silverlight.  I hope that this is good news to the implementers of media and Silverlight.  You can find out more information about the <a href="http://www.ibc.org">IBC here</a> and a full <a href="http://www.microsoft.com/presspass/features/2008/sep08/09-09silverlight.mspx">Q&amp;A with ScottGu</a> here which also gives some great information about the results of viewing at NBCOlympics.com and where other uses of Windows Media Server were implemented (CCTV for example).</p>
<p>UPDATE: To clarify, this support will be in a future version of Silverlight.<br />
</p>
