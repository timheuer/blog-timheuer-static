<p><a href="http://www.microsoft.com/mexico/mixessentials" target="_new"><img style="margin: 0px 10px 0px 0px" height="120" alt="I'm Speaking at Mix Essentials - La Esencia de la Web - 5 de junio, 2008, México, D.F." src="http://www.microsoft.com/mexico/mixessentials/images/banner_speaking_at_2esp.jpg" width="280" align="left" border="0" /></a> This week I’ll be traveling to Mexico City to meet developers and designers at the local <a href="http://www.microsoft.com/mexico/mixessentials">MIX Essentials</a> (<a href="http://www.windowslivetranslator.com/BV.aspx?#http://www.microsoft.com/mexico/mixessentials/agenda.aspx">English translation here</a> – funny that RIA gets translated to LAUGHS) event.  I’ll be presenting two sessions there this week.  I was fortunate enough to be asked to kick-off the conference talking about building RIAs with <a href="http://silverlight.net">Silverlight</a> 2.  I’m particularly excited about this as I have some things to show that haven’t really been shown broadly yet.  I will also be speaking on working with data in Silverlight 2 later in the day.  This session will cover working with services, servers, sockets and LINQ as a part of Silverlight development.</p>  <p>I hope that all those near Mexico City and around will be able to join us at this event.  If you are planning on being there, I look forward to meeting you and getting feedback on Silverlight as well as seeing what really interesting things are being developed in Mexico.  See you soon!</p>  <div class="wlWriterSmartContent" id="scid:0767317B-992E-4b12-91E0-4F059A8CECA8:f88bc6d1-c47f-454d-96ce-3a8deca84eb2" style="padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-left: 0px; float: none; padding-bottom: 0px; margin: 0px; padding-top: 0px"></div>
