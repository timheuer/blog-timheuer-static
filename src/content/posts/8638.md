<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face=Arial size=2>TOPLINE: Code Camp comes to the desert May 6 at University of Advancing Technology (Phoenix, AZ)!&nbsp; Register now: </FONT><A href="http://www.desertcodecamp.com/"><U><FONT face=Arial color=#800080 size=2>http://www.desertcodecamp.com</FONT></U></A><FONT face=Arial size=2> Please forward to your developer customer contacts.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><?xml:namespace prefix = o /><o:p><FONT face=Arial size=2>&nbsp;</FONT></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face=Arial size=2>What is a Code Camp?&nbsp; In short, it is a <B>free developer conference</B>.&nbsp; Who is welcome? Anyone!!!!!</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><o:p><FONT face=Arial size=2>&nbsp;</FONT></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face=Arial size=2>To get a better understanding, here&#8217;s the Code Camp Manifesto:</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><B><FONT size=2><FONT face=Arial>By and For the Developer Community<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></B></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face=Arial size=2>Code Camps are about the developer community at large. They are meant to be a place for developers to come and learn from their peers. Topics are always based on community interest and never determined by anyone other than the community.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><B><FONT size=2><FONT face=Arial>Always Free<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></B></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face=Arial size=2>Code Camps are always free for attendees.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><B><FONT size=2><FONT face=Arial>Community Developed Material<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></B></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face=Arial size=2>The success of the Code Camps is that they are based on community content. All content that is delivered is original. All presentation content must be provided completely (including code) without any restriction. If you have content you don't want to share or provide to attendees then the Code Camp is not the place for you.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><B><FONT size=2><FONT face=Arial>No Fluff only Code<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></B></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face=Arial size=2>Code Camps are about showing the code. Refer to rule #1 if you have any questions on this. </FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><B><FONT size=2><FONT face=Arial>Community Ownership<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></B></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face=Arial size=2>The most important element of the Code Camp is always the developer community. All are welcome to attend and speak and do so without expectation of payment or any other compensation other than their participation in the community.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><B><FONT size=2><FONT face=Arial>Never occur during work hours<o:p></o:p></FONT></FONT></B></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face=Arial size=2>We need to understand that many times people can't leave work for a day or two to attend training or even seminars. The beauty of the Code Camp is that they always occur on weekends.</FONT></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><o:p><FONT face=Arial size=2>&nbsp;</FONT></o:p></P>
<P class=MsoNormal style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt"><FONT face=Arial size=2>The community leaders in the desert have worked together to arrange this venue for our software developer communities.&nbsp; It&#8217;s not a &#8220;one-technology&#8221; event.&nbsp; There will be sessions on Java, Flash, open source, Microsoft, database, and all other technologies.&nbsp; Nothing is off limits.</FONT></P>
