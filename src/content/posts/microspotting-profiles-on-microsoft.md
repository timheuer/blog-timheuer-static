<p><a title="Ian Ellison-Taylor" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/48889089289@N01/2106266756/"><img style="margin: 0px 10px 0px 0px" alt="Ian Ellison-Taylor" src="http://static.flickr.com/2182/2106266756_475aa1420e_m.jpg" align="left" border="0" /></a>I’ve been watching Ariel’s blog for a while and really like the insight.  What is it? <a href="http://www.microspotting.com/">Microspotting</a>, where she describes “Like the paparazzi, but for geeks.”</p>  <p>I would say similar to the core tenet of Channel 9, <strong>Microspotting</strong> has the intent of turning the looking glass inward to get some profiles of key individuals you might recognize within <strong>Microsoft</strong>.  What is great is that the story is augmented by another of Ariel’s talents: photography.  She’s pretty good and I’ve enjoyed the photos that accompany the profiles and stories.</p>  <p>From her site:</p>  <p><em>My name is Ariel and Microspotting is the place where I collect my photos and ramblings about the supposedly-Evil Empire and what it’s really like to work here.Now I creep around campus talking to my favorite kinds of people: geeks who are passionate about what they do.</em></p>  <p>Some of my favorite profiles:</p>  <ul>   <ul>     <li><a href="http://www.microspotting.com/2008/05/jeff-sandquist">Jeff Sandquist – evangelists vs. apologists</a></li>      <li><a href="http://www.microspotting.com/2008/05/not-all-softies-believe-in-business-casual">Jonathan Biderman – not all softies are biz casual</a></li>      <li><a href="http://www.microspotting.com/2008/02/peruvian-rockstar-sdet">Miguel Susffalich – Peruvian rock star or SDET?</a></li>      <li><a href="http://www.microspotting.com/2008/01/goth-in-the-office">Jillian Venters – the goth in the office</a></li>   </ul> </ul>  <p>I’m sure I’m not high-profile enough (or geographically desirable for a profile anyway), but if I was, I could see a photo shoot with massive beads of sweat rolling down my forehead as I lean against a saguaro cactus…or maybe just an office shot?</p>  <p>Anyway, it’s an interesting insight into not only developers, but interesting profiles into Microsoft culture.  Check out <a href="http://www.microspotting.com/">Microspotting</a>.</p>  <div
