<p>so i, along with probably others ;-), took a look at <a href="http://calendar.google.com/">google calendar</a>.&nbsp; why?&nbsp; it&rsquo;s hard to argue they&rsquo;ve come out with some cool stuff.&nbsp; one thing that my wife and i struggle with is maintaining a calendar we can see for each other.&nbsp; </p>
<p>yeah, i know about all the other sites, etc. &mdash; why haven&rsquo;t i used them?&nbsp; who knows&hellip;just haven&rsquo;t.&nbsp; i looked at hotmail, and while i think the Windows Live team is making great improvements (Windows Live Mail is great), i&rsquo;m still frustrated with the passport authentication model.&nbsp; with everything i do in software, i try (not always succeeding) to apply the MILF design principle.&nbsp; no, not that one.&nbsp; MILF=mother-in-law factor.&nbsp; for me, passport doesn&rsquo;t pass this test (and has been proven by field MILF studies ;-) &mdash; and numerous family &lsquo;nick burns&rsquo; visits).</p>
<p>at any rate, i looked at google calendar.&nbsp; slick design, web 2.0, ajax, etc. &mdash; &lt;insert buzzword here&gt; &mdash; it&rsquo;s pretty slick.&nbsp; i set up a calendar and shared it out to my wife with a note about my idea.&nbsp; within 10 minutes, the month of april was completed with all relevant items for our family that i needed to be aware of.&nbsp; seriously, this is a great user experience achievement in my book.&nbsp; while my wife is progressing in the tech savvy department, some things still need some explaining.&nbsp; however, this is where i think google succeeds well.&nbsp; she has been using gmail for a while and loves it (she would never touch her hotmail because she said it was too &lsquo;busy&rsquo;).&nbsp; google calendar was easy for her to use as well.</p>
<p>so i knew outlook 2007 (outlook 12 for those still fighting the naming conventions) had the ability to read iCal formats.&nbsp; google calendar publishes in that format.&nbsp; i figured, great! now i can see my wife/family appointments in <strong>my</strong> outlook!</p>

<p>note: i tried this with my new windows live account (if you have hotmail you should be able to use http://calendar.msn.com/&lt;yourhotmailaccount&gt;) but didn&rsquo;t have any results.&nbsp; i may not have done the config right or hotmail calendar doesn&rsquo;t support ical format&hellip;i haven&rsquo;t researched but it just didn&rsquo;t work for me as quickly as google calendar did.</p>
<p>anyhow, very interesting to see how google continues to suceed with advanced simplicity.</p>
<p><strong>UPDATE: here&rsquo;s how I did it&hellip;</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Go to Google Calendar and click Settings, then click Calendars and click on the calendar you want to view, you should be at the Calendar Details screen like this</li>
<li><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/timheuer/168285910/"><img alt="gcal1" src="http://static.flickr.com/53/168285910_0fda5a7c97.jpg" border="0" /></a></li>
<li>Click on the Private Address ICAL button and copy the URL that is displayed.</li>
<li>Go to Outlook 2007, go to Tools menu, choose Account Settings, and then the Internet Calendar tab</li>
<li><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/timheuer/168285904/"><img alt="gcal2" src="http://static.flickr.com/58/168285904_ba8291c4da.jpg" border="0" /></a></li>
<li>Click New and paste the URL you copied there (no need to change it to webcal://)</li></ul>
<p>That is what worked for me, others have reported doing the same things and not working, but mine worked &mdash; it will be read-only calendar viewing.</p>
