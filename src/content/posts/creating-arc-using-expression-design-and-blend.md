<p>while perusing the <a href="http://silverlight.net/forums">silverlight.net forums</a> i found a question of "<a href="http://silverlight.net/forums/p/4751/14266.aspx#14266">creating half circle in blend</a>" and posted my answer (actually posted the xaml of my answer).</p>
<p><a href="http://channel9.msdn.com/ShowPost.aspx?PostID=342171"><img style="MARGIN: 0px 5px 0px 0px" alt="Manipulating Expression" align="left" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/timheuer-img/arc-title-small.png" /></a>rather than try to articulate it in text, which i started out doing and was failing in my own mind, i decided to do a quick screencast demonstrating what i did.  keep in mind i'm not a vector guy but did it the way i knew how.  i hope that someone can correct me with a super easier way of doing it, but until then i'm fine with my method :-).  essentially i used expression <a href="http://www.microsoft.com/Expression/products/download.aspx?key=design">design</a> to do most of the manipulation of creating the elements/images i would use in <a href="http://www.microsoft.com/Expression/products/download.aspx?key=blend2preview">blend</a>.  i think blend does well for some simple element tasks, but if you want to do some more complex things (not that this is a complex example, just making a point), you might be better served in design.  you can view this screencast by subscribing to my podcast feeds on the left area of my blog.</p>
