<p>If you’re a pro <a href="http://silverlight.net"><strong>Silverlight</strong></a> developer, this post isn’t for you.  Just a brief update that I’ve updated some of our getting started material for beginners – those who really haven’t done anything.  These will be showing up on the <a href="http://silverlight.net"><strong>Silverlight Community Site</strong></a> soon, but I wanted to post a link to it here first.</p>  <p><strong><a href="http://timheuer.com/blog/articles/getting-started-with-silverlight-development.aspx">Getting Started with Silverlight Development</a></strong> is a 7-part series where I aim to do my best in trying to stuff as much as possible about Silverlight development in a simple application we develop at different steps.  It uses:</p>  <ul>   <li>Navigation</li>    <li>Styles/templates</li>    <li>Data Templates</li>    <li>Data binding</li>    <li>Value Converters</li>    <li><a href="http://silverlight.codeplex.com"><strong>Silverlight Toolkit</strong></a> controls</li>    <li>Isolated Storage</li>    <li>Network connectivity</li>    <li>Web service requests to a public service</li>    <li>Out-of-browser experiences</li> </ul>  <p>I hope that it is simple enough for beginners to follow but also provide a broad spectrum of capabilities to the beginner.  Full code in C# and Visual Basic is provided.</p>  <p>Hope this helps!</p>  <div style="padding-bottom: 0px; margin: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; float: none; padding-top: 0px" id="scid:0767317B-992E-4b12-91E0-4F059A8CECA8:aed3700c-69a7-49ea-b9c2-58860606c235" class="wlWriterEditableSmartContent"></div>
