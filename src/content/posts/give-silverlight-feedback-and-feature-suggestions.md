<p>I’ve been doing a lot of asking and listening lately…to you, the <a href="http://silverlight.net"><strong>Silverlight</strong></a> developers!  I’ve received a lot of positive feedback and “thank you for asking” comments and it has been real encouraging.  If you haven’t seen some of them, here’s some of my recent inquiries:</p>  <ul>   <li><a href="http://twtpoll.com/knuzik">Do you have a need to convert Flash applications</a> (not just assets, but ActionScript)?</li>    <li><a href="http://timheuer.com/blog/archive/2009/09/03/how-do-you-want-to-learn-silverlight.aspx">How do you prefer to learn Silverlight</a> (and how do you decide on choosing Silverlight)?</li>    <li><a href="http://twtpoll.com/60otvy">Are you using the provided Silverlight Toolkit themes in your applications</a>?</li> </ul>  <ul>If you have thoughts on these, please share them at the links above.  We’ve also been capturing ways of looking for feature suggestions.  One experiment has been using a ‘wisdom of crowds’ type approach.  Previously <a href="http://blogs.msdn.com/jstegman"><strong>Joe Stegman</strong></a> (group director for the Silverlight runtime) had spelunked the forum questions, blogs, etc.  Check out what he has to say (emphasis mine) about a recent effort to collect some suggestion feedback:</ul>  <blockquote>   <p><em><strong>Tim Heuer</strong> and </em><a href="http://blogs.msdn.com/msmossyblog"><strong><em>Scott Barnes</em></strong></a><em> have established a more community focused way of tracking Silverlight 4 feature requests.  You can find their feature tracker here: </em><a href="http://silverlight.uservoice.com/"><em>Silverlight Feedback Forum</em></a><em>.   Other than a few items that our outside our control (iPhone, bundling), <strong>you're likely to see at least a few of the top items in the next version of Silverlight</strong>. source: </em><a href="http://blogs.msdn.com/jstegman/archive/2009/09/08/silverlight-4-feature-requests.aspx"><em>Joe Stegman</em></a><em>.</em></p> </blockquote>  <p>Hmm…some hints to the future?! :-)</p>  <ul>Thanks for all your feedback and keep it coming!   <div style="padding-bottom: 0px; margin: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; float: none; padding-top: 0px"
