<p>Thanks for all the feedback on the TechEd North America DVD issues with viewing the content once <a href="http://silverlight.net">Silverlight</a> 2 released.&nbsp; In trying to do a good job providing a good user experience to viewing the content easily, the TechEd team created a Silverlight 2 Beta 2 player for the TechEd Online site as well as the offline DVD content.</p>
<p>Obviously, when Silverlight 2 released and people installed the final version on their machines, the DVD Silverlight player stopped working and presented you with an &ldquo;Install Silverlight&rdquo; badge.&nbsp; This was, of course, due to the use of Beta 2.&nbsp; This doesn&rsquo;t render the DVD set useless as the content is still there, but you just aren&rsquo;t able to use the Silverlight player from the DVD.</p>
<p>The team has updated the player in the online version of the content already.&nbsp; Unfortunately you can&rsquo;t service burned DVDs (aside from burning new sets and sending them to the thousands of people, which isn&rsquo;t efficient, cost effective or environmentally friendly), but we can help you understand the maze of content folders.&nbsp; We&rsquo;ve finalized the document that maps out where the content files are as well as providing you with a &ldquo;content map&rdquo; of the codes to the session descriptions and the speakers who delivered the sessions.&nbsp; This list was made based on your feedback and the team tried to include as much information as they could to make it easy to discover.&nbsp; You can download a PDF copy of this content map here: <a href="http://s3.amazonaws.com:80/timheuer-img/techeddvdmap.pdf">TechEd 2008 North America DVD content map</a>.</p>
<p>I&rsquo;ve also taken the liberty to make a quick Silverlight application to help you do some quick filtering of the data.&nbsp; It&rsquo;s uploaded into <a href="http://silverlight.live.com">Silverlight Streaming</a> so you can embed it into your site or as well into your Intranet if your employees or others in your organization are needing to navigate the DVD set as well.&nbsp; You can select which conference (Developer or IT) and also type some filter in there that will search the speaker/title and filter it down.</p>

<p>In hindsight, choosing to burn a copy of a Beta 2 application to a distributable disc was not a good idea.&#160; We admit that and apologize.&#160; Hopefully you can see that the content is the king here and that is not lost or unusable.&#160; Thanks for your patience while we created the content map based on your feedback!</p>
