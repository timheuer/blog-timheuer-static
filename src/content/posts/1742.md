<P>i've been thinking recently about beta software.&nbsp; i've recently been trying to learn why some companies, already heavily invested in .net technologies wouldn't rapidly adopt .net beta 2.&nbsp; i mean, it isn't drastically different (as was the perception of the .net framework from previous windna platform technologies)...it's still .net -- just a better .net.</P>
<P>in my conversations with individuals and companies alike, mostly the response has been &#8220;it's still beta [dude]&#8221; (dude added in for the individual response.&nbsp; okay, yeah, you're right it is.&nbsp; but what if i told you that ms offers a go-live license (as they have done in the past) so that you can comfortably go-live with the software in a production environment?&nbsp; would that make you change your mind?&nbsp; i mean, that's a pretty huge confidence mark to say &#8220;go ahead and use our beta in production&#8221; don't you think?</P>
<P>think for a minute driving directions.&nbsp; if you have NEVER BEEN TO A LOCATION before, you're likely going to get some driving directions -- and people usually get them from an online source.&nbsp; google maps has won out recently as one of the &#8220;coolest&#8221; map/direction provider.&nbsp; and i'm sure a <STRIKE>ton</STRIKE> zilliton&nbsp;of people use it daily for maps/directions.&nbsp; the next time you do...take a look in the upper left corner...see that -- bingo...that magic word...&#8221;beta&#8221; right there in front of you.</P>
<P>if you look further at google's site, they indicate that it is still in beta -- and their definition &#8220;translation: more improvements to come&#8221; is a pretty solid statement about their confidence...akin to saying &#8220;go ahead and use our stuff in production.&#8221;</P>
<P>so, you'll rely on beta software to get you from point a to point b, provided you've never been to point b, but you wouldn't even CONSIDER using it for your efforts in software development?&nbsp; to me that's quite ironic (note: i didn't say stupid or dumb or hypocrytical...just ironic).</P>
<P><EM>but tim, google beta works.</EM> touche.&nbsp; ms has a history of beta software being what beta used to really mean (translation: hey man, this stuff is beta, install and cross your fingers).&nbsp; the builds are slow, functions missing, functions not working, etc.&nbsp; i mean <STRONG>*real*</STRONG> beta.&nbsp; but we're getting better.&nbsp; thus the slip in beta2 for .net -- a release strategy (even for beta) built on quality, not a schedule.&nbsp; ms is redifining (i'm interpreting here of course, nothing official) their beta software definition...to adapt to a better expectation of what is released into the wild.</P>
<P>so, come on...give a look, find a project to skunkworks, or do a little pet project on building a beta2 environment and running your current apps on it...give our &#8220;directions&#8221; a chance and use them...you've relied on .net up till now, why not look and see what is <STRONG><EM>better</EM></STRONG> about it?</P>
