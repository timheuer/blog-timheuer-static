<P><A href="http://www.microsoft.com/presspass/exec/rick/default.mspx" rel=tag>rick rashid</A> from the <A href="http://research.microsoft.com/" rel=tag>microsoft research</A> group just came to speak to the group.&nbsp; this wasn&#8217;t the typical technology presentation you are used to and there really wasn&#8217;t any intention to have &#8220;open source&#8221; discussions around research ;-), but to show what microsoft does to invest in technology in an altruistic manner and publish work we are doing.</P>
<P>rick&#8217;s history comes from MOC among other things and if you are using a mac, you are using code he wrote 23 years ago as he says.</P>
<P>probably the coolest things that rick showed was the research they did in image rendering/editing/whatever.&nbsp; rick showed the <A href="http://research.microsoft.com/projects/GroupShot/" rel=tag>groupshot</A> feature which enables you to pick the best parts of various photos and stitch them together in a &#8220;best photo&#8221; for yourself.</P>
<P>the other image research he showed was stuff like literally taking any photo, highlighting things you don&#8217;t want in it and having it completely removed from the photo, but not leaving any voids and having original photos appear that nothing was missing &#8212; literally making it look like that person that walked by in the middle of your photo was never there&#8230;truly amazing.</P>
<P>he also showed technology by taking a 2D image and being able to move it around to appear to look at the image in 3D &#8212; with using the single image &#8212; not additional images&#8230;it was almost frightening.</P>
<P>but probably the coolest thing was a minority report-esque glass wall&#8230;remember the part in that movie where tom cruise moved images, etc. around with his hands jut by pointing at the glass screen?&nbsp; yeah, that&#8217;s in the microsoft museum now&#8230;friggin unreal.&nbsp; i snapped a vid of jack herrington playing with it here.</P>

<P>the important thing to note is that msft research is a basic research organization &#8212; which means academic.&nbsp; their stuff is amazing and rick has said they plan on trying to publish more to the open public&#8230;</P>
