<p>at the phoenix barcamp i had an opportunity to look at the new nintendo wii console.  the facility has just about every console (except an xbox 360...hmmm).  some of the guys from <a href="http://www.integrumtech.com" rel="tag">integrum technologies</a> had one in their office and were raving about it so they showed me the ropes.</p> <p>i have to say, i was quite impressed.  the graphics looked...hmmm...like nintendo if that makes sense...i still felt i was in super mario land versus some street in project gotham...but i guess it is a different target audience.</p> <p>playing around with the remote was different...it's a mouse essentially communicating with an infrared bar you put somewhere near your television.  then it sense motion for certain games (we were playing the wii sports: tennis, boxing, golf) and makes the players do whatever.  it worked remarkably well and in the boxing game could give you a bit of a workout even.</p> <p>the integrum guys commented about making sure we attached the remote to our wrists using the safety tether.  and apparently looking at this site: <a title="http://www.wiihaveaproblem.com/show_cat.php?cat=2" href="http://www.wiihaveaproblem.com/show_cat.php?cat=2">http://www.wiihaveaproblem.com/show_cat.php?cat=2</a> that needs to be stressed :-)</p> <p>it's no xbox 360 in terms of graphics quality or depth of games i don't think, but it is a different audience.</p> <div class="wlWriterSmartContent" id="0767317B-992E-4b12-91E0-4F059A8CECA8:7ad5b658-6ef5-4edc-9025-70921541a43e" contenteditable="false" style="padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-left: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; margin: 0px; padding-top: 0px"></div>
