<p>well it looks like the &lt;possiblyrenamed&gt;Phone from Apple might hit some snags.  first there is the lawsuite filed by cisco (<a href="http://blogs.cisco.com/news/2007/01/update_on_ciscos_iphone_tradem.html">good commentary from cisco general counsel here</a>) about the trademark violation, and then the news is starting to trickle out.</p> <p>first, the "OS X" maybe isn't so OS X as promised.  according to a VP Q&amp;A session it is similar to how iPod uses OS X...a psuedo subset with no open APIs.  this likely may mean that <a href="http://www.tuaw.com/2007/01/09/iphone-will-not-allow-user-installable-applications/">no user-installable applications will be allowed</a>...snag for Apple in my opinion.</p> <p>the screen also uses the same polycarb as the ipod -- and remember the reports of screen scratching and durability?  hmmm...wonder if this will have similar issues given the surface area.  wifi syncing -- nope.  what?!  well according to apple, nope.  blah.</p> <div class="wlWriterSmartContent" id="0767317B-992E-4b12-91E0-4F059A8CECA8:ec618da2-6c6f-49c0-9e55-ba9f48a9d656" contenteditable="false" style="padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-left: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; margin: 0px; padding-top: 0px"></div>
