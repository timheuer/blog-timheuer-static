<p>well after installing subtext and lurking around, i decided i was sick of the “designed by tech” skins.  don’t get me wrong, most of them were good, but let’s be honest…they are designed by non-designers most of the time.</p>
<p>after hunting around, i realized that i’d been playing around with ruby on rails lately and as well with typo.  i remembered that an author had created a pretty cool theme for typo called ‘origami’ — well i downloaded it, deciphered all the references and created my swag at a subtext skin based on that.</p>
<p>lo and behold, what you are viewing is it.  (note: if you are viewing/reading this through an rss/news/feed aggregator/reader, there is nothing to see here, move along).  </p>
<p>if you want it, you can download it: <a href="http://codegallery.gotdotnet.com/origami" rel="tag">origami skin for subtext</a></p>
<p>there are three files in the zip — one of them is clearly labeled README.  if you don’t follow the steps and email me, i’m going to send you moldy bread from my fridge…seriously please read.</p>
<p>i’m making some modifications to subtext to include better tagging visibility and may put those up soon.</p>
<p>have fun!</p>
