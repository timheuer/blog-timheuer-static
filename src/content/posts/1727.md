<P>i've been traveling a bit lately...and officially decided i hate the airports...</P>
<BLOCKQUOTE dir=ltr style="MARGIN-RIGHT: 0px">
<P>disclaimer: i firmly believe in ensuring the safety of everyone when traveling</P></BLOCKQUOTE>
<P dir=ltr>i think the random screening process needs to be examined and the algorithm fixed...i kid you not, i saw an elderly gentleman who could barely walk get the full security check/screen/pat down/search/etc.&nbsp;meanwhile me with 2 laptops, pda, smartphone, backpack full of cds, two wireless devices, cables, etc. has no problem getting through.</P>
<P dir=ltr>oh yeah and try getting through the security screen now...i followed all the posted rules, took my laptop(s) out and in a spearate container, stripped myself of anything metallic-ish and proceeded toward the metal detector, only to be greeted by the &#8220;security officer&#8221; putting his hand up ala diana ross 'stop in the name of love' pose.</P>
<BLOCKQUOTE dir=ltr style="MARGIN-RIGHT: 0px">
<P dir=ltr>TSA: &#8220;Sir, I'm going to suggest you remove your shoes and put them through the scanner&#8221;</P>
<P dir=ltr>Me: &#8220;Do I have to?&#8221;</P>
<P dir=ltr>TSA: &#8220;No, but then you will have the full screening.&#8221;</P>
<P dir=ltr>Me (thinking): Then why don't you just tell me to rather than suggest it as an option--OR POST A NEW RULE!</P></BLOCKQUOTE>
<P dir=ltr>keep in mind i wasn't wearing steel-toed doc martens or anything i would deem any &#8220;clunky&#8221; at all -- even the pa announcement system was saying shoes with soles over 1 inch should be removed...</P>
<P dir=ltr>and i'm pretty sure this is what you have to wear these days to bypass the &#8220;suggestion&#8221;:</P>
