<p><strong>UPDATE</strong>: <a href="http://timheuer.com/blog/archive/2007/07/23/14152.aspx">ironruby sourcecode released</a>!</p> <p>this week i'm heading to one of my favorite cities, portland.  it is the week the open source geeks descend upon the oregon convention center for a week of open source-ness for <a href="http://conferences.oreillynet.com/os2007/">OSCON</a>.</p> <p>so why am i going?  i went last year and had a blast...and learned a lot.  this year i'm going to lurk a little bit more, try to get some candid thoughts of attendees and hopefully learn some more!</p> <p>microsoft will have a good showing this year, and i'm most excited about john lam's session on ironruby.  if you are headed to oscon, please be sure to <a href="http://conferences.oreillynet.com/cs/os2007/view/e_sess/14280">check it out on thursday @ 5:20pm</a> (btw, why do FOSS conferences go so late into the night!? -- or i'm just getting old and lazy).  john will talk about dynamic languages on .net -- ironruby, the BSD-style project for implementing ruby on .net, and probably show some silverlight madness implementing these dynamic languages.</p> <p>see you there!  if you are going, you can <a href="http://twitter.com/timheuer">follow me on twitter</a>, or ring me up on my cell, email, whatever...let's hook up and chat!  i've put my list on <a href="http://myconfplan.com/conferences/OSCON+2007/users/timheuer">myconfplan.com</a> of where i think i'll be (dang, there is another session during john's that i really want to go to!).</p> <div class="wlWriterSmartContent" id="scid:0767317B-992E-4b12-91E0-4F059A8CECA8:23f31347-4239-4476-a566-b941ee49473f" style="padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-left: 0px; float: none; padding-bottom: 0px; margin: 0px; padding-top: 0px"></div>
