<p>i just saw this pop in my inbox as a note (not to me) to one of the local user groups i belong to:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>I have been a member of this group now for some time, and while I am not yet <br />
at the level of many in here, I have greatly benefited from the help I have <br />
received. I just wanted to say, in the spirit of Thanksgiving, thanks to <br />
all those who have answered my numerous questions, and made my programming <br />
experiences all the better. Have a great Thanksgiving everyone!</p>
</blockquote>
<p>this is great, this is exactly the type of feedback that validates all the hard work from the leaders in community in gathering like-minded people together.  if you aren't involved in your local community...do it now.  look at <a href="http://www.codezone.com">www.codezone.com</a> to see if there is a .net community near you or reach out to your local microsoft office to find out who the community evangelist is in your area.</p>
<p>it isn't just for softies as well...interested in learning new technologies or broadening your network of talent?  find a ruby user group, find a php user group...bottom line: get involved.  even the silent ones involved benefit.  this note above came from a list that has grown over the past 9 years from about 20 to hanging steadily at about 1800.  it is one of the groups i'm most proud to be a member.</p>
<p>happy holidays!</p>
