<p><strong>UPDATE: visit <font face="Arial"><a href="http://timheuer.com/blog/archive/2006/10/01/13547.aspx">http://timheuer.com/blog/archive/2006/10/01/13547.aspx</a> for the link to download SnopeIt!</font></strong></p>
<p>if you've installed the <a rel="tag" href="http://timheuer.com/blog/archive/2006/09/19/13475.aspx">visual studio tools for office beta 2 second edition</a> (man everytime i read our product names, it hurts), you'll notice that when you create an add-in project, it automatically creates a setup project along side it for you -- great idea (since you'll need a method to deploy it anyway).</p>
<p>when i <a href="http://timheuer.com/blog/archive/2006/09/19/13478.aspx">started to play around with the vsto tools and my SnopeIt add-in</a>, the installer wasn't working for me.  it deployed fine, but outlook was giving a runtime load error.  after some investigation i figured it was something with security.  some colleagues were kind enough to point me to a solution.</p>
<p>an article on msdn titled '<a rel="tag" href="http://msdn.microsoft.com/library/default.asp?url=/library/en-us/odc_vsto2003_tr/html/odc_VSTMGBLab5.asp">Deploying Office Documents that Use Managed Code Extensions</a>' outlines a setup helper class (among one method) to implement the trust mechanism required for the add-in.  I literally did a cut/paste of the code in a SetupHelper assembly, plopped it in to my setup custom actions and it worked great.</p>
<p>one thing i did have to change was the CustomActionData.  it indicated something like:</p>
<div class="CodeFormatContainer">
<div class="csharpcode">
<pre><span class="lnum">   1:  </span>/path=<span class="str">"[TARGETDIR]My Word Bookmarks_bin/My Word Bookmarks.dll"</span></pre>
</div>
</div>
<p> </p>
<p>i think the article was written for office 2003 add-ins and there may be a different deployment model hence the _bin folder.  i found it wasn't necessary and just changed it to (changing to your assembly of course):</p>
<div class="CodeFormatContainer">
<div class="csharpcode">
<pre><span class="lnum">   1:  </span>/path=<span class="str">"[TARGETDIR]My Word Bookmarks.dll"</span></pre>
</div>
</div>
<p> </p>
<p>this worked great for me.  so now that i have that worked out, here is the <a href="http://www.box.net/public/ladlq4tsvc">download link to the rough cut version</a> of my simple SnopeIt add-in.  it simply launches snopes.com with the subject line -- like i said, simple.  i'm using this to learn more about the office 2007 development model though and will adjust when i get more time to some more features...any suggestions?</p>
