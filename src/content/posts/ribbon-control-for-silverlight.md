<p>A company called <a href="http://www.divelements.co.uk/net/">divelements</a> has had Windows Forms products for a while that produced, among other things, the “docking” effect that you see with some applications, including Visual Studio.  This is the ability to drag a window to another area and have it automatically dock to that particular position of the parent window:</p>  <p><img style="margin: 0px 10px 0px 0px; display: inline" align="left" src="http://storage.timheuer.com/divdock3.png" />This product is also available for WPF and for <a href="http://silverlight.net">Silverlight</a>.  So if you need to add this type of dynamic window moving to your applications, you can check out the <a href="http://divelements.co.uk/net/controls/sanddocksl/livedemo.aspx">SandDock for Silverlight controls</a>. </p>  <p>What is great is that in looking at what else they provide, I was sent a demonstration link to a Ribbon interface, similar to that of Office 2007 that is becoming popular among Windows application these days.  When I saw this demonstration I felt like I was looking at some weird hack or something…it was so strikingly similar to the Office interface and <em>in Silverlight</em>.  I think organizations looking to have a line-of-business application with a familiar interface to what their users probably are already seeing is a good thing…it keeps a sense of familiarity in front of their face.</p>  <p>I’ve come to learn that divelements was one of the first companies to <a href="http://msdn2.microsoft.com/en-us/office/aa973809.aspx">license the Ribbon UI interface</a>.  I think they may have actually helped shape enabling this use for other component vendors as well.  Take a look at their Silverlight Ribbon:</p>  <p><img src="http://divelements.co.uk/net/controls/sandribbonsl/snapshot-topleft.gif" /> </p>  <p>That’s Silverlight in a familiar and functional user interface.  Pretty cool.  Their sample really made me think I was operating a full client application!  The <a href="http://divelements.co.uk/net/controls/sandribbonsl/">SandRibbon for Silverlight</a> is currently undergoing beta use right now and they have a link on their site to request to try it out.</p>  <p>It is so great to see this ecosystem of Silverlight controls emerge so quickly for us developers to take advantage of and create compelling user interfaces and line-of-business applications in a consistent and productive manner.</p>  <div style="padding-bottom: 0px;
