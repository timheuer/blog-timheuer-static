<p>everytime i travel i seem to find myself ranting on wireless access. i'm in seattle. they don't have free wireless.</p>
<p>with <a href="http://www.wazmetro.com/">cities making wireless more of a utility in their neighborhoods</a>, it got me thinking about public access.&nbsp; i <a href="http://timheuer.com/blog/archive/2006/03/08/8358.aspx">recently switched to verizon</a> and use their evdo data network.&nbsp; now at seattle, i don&rsquo;t have to complain anymore (although i found a 1xRTT spot and EVDO spot about 20 feet away from each other &mdash; weird).&nbsp; no free wireless, no problem, plug in my <a href="http://www.palm.com/us/products/smartphones/treo700w/?creativeID=HmPg_BB|700w_verizon">treo</a> with my <a href="http://www.boxwave.com/products/bundles/carcharger/palm-treo-700w-car-charger-bundle_1043.htm">mini-sync</a> (which is charging it at the same time), connect w/<a href="http://www.junefabrics.com/pdanet/">pdanet</a> and i have high speed wireless (free? of course not, i&rsquo;m paying for the data plan on my phone).</p>
<p>this got me thinking to my normal rants on wireless in airports.&nbsp; i think the airlines should step in.&nbsp; in my area i usually have a choice between 2&ndash;3 low cost carriers.&nbsp; i choose <a href="http://www.americawest.com/">one</a> because i&rsquo;m a <a href="http://timheuer.com/blog/articles/FultonHomesSuckPart2.aspx">loyal</a> customer.&nbsp; but it got me thinking.&nbsp; would i choose the <a href="http://www.southwest.com/">other</a> if they offered free wireless in their terminal areas?&nbsp; i have to say i&rsquo;d think about it more given long travel wait times these days.&nbsp; i think airlines have an opportunity here to increase their competitive offerings.&nbsp; offer free wireless in your terminal areas.&nbsp; maybe even offer a few kiosk terminals for the people without machines.</p>
<p>oh, and the lightening&hellip;that&rsquo;s what is allowing me to write this post.&nbsp; my plane is delayed as coming inbound it was struck by lightening.&nbsp; would you reboard this plane?</p>
<p><strong>gate attendant</strong>: &ldquo;we&rsquo;re holding off boarding because the plane was struck by lightening coming into seattle.&rdquo;<br /><strong>me (thinking):</strong> huh, i guess i can at least apreciate them being honest, but it doesn&rsquo;t leave the happiest feeling as i have no idea what lightening does to a plane&hellip;i have a pretty good idea what it does to trees, humans, houses, etc.<br /><em>20 minutes pass&hellip;<br /></em><strong>gate attendant</strong>: &ldquo;we appreciate your patience.&nbsp; it appears there is a chunk out of the aft tail.&nbsp; it looks like an animal bite and they are having some engineers take a look at the plane&rsquo;s schematics to see if it is affected.&rdquo;<br /><strong>me (thinking):</strong> animal bite chunk?&nbsp; hmm&hellip;that doesn&rsquo;t sound good.<br /><em>(looking out window of gate): looks like they called in a specialist from a competing airline</em><br /><strong>me (thinking and still looking out window)</strong>: what does it mean when all the engineers walk away from the plane?&nbsp; my guess: animal bite chunk != this plane leaving.</p>
<p>updated: plain scheduled to leave 17:15.&nbsp; plane will be leaving 23:20 &mdash; nice.&nbsp; off to find a cozy place to sit and wifi-away!</p>
