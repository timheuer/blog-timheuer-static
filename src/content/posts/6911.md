<P>in <A href="http://maps.msn.com/map.aspx?C=33.24457,-111.6345&amp;L=USA&amp;A=50&amp;S=405,320&amp;PN=1151959658&amp;P=%7c2f073%7c&amp;TI=Queen+Creek%2c+Arizona%2c+United+States">my neck of the woods</A>, <A href="http://www.cableamerica.com/">cableamerica</A> is a provider of cable, internet and internet phone services.&nbsp; it is one of <A href="http://www.cableamerica.com/">basically</A> <A href="http://www.cox.net/">three</A> <A href="http://www.qwest.com/">choices</A>.&nbsp; however, in my opinion, it is the best.</P>
<P>i jumped on board with them early on and they are a relatively small organization not trying to bite off huge bits of the market &#8212; early on they resold at&amp;t broadband basically.&nbsp; their prices are great, their services is superb (read: responsive, technical, helpful), and their technical service offerings are awesome (no ports blocked, static ips, &#8216;we don&#8217;t care what you do&#8217; mentality, great hd service on tv, etc.).&nbsp; this has helped in past employment as a remote worker (vpn back into home, various client email settings and not having to change them, etc.) and has been a great experience to me.</P>
<P>now i read that <A href="http://www.bizjournals.com/phoenix/stories/2006/01/09/daily27.html">cox is buying cableamerica operations</A> that affect me.&nbsp; this concerns me for several reasons.&nbsp; first, change generally scares some people.&nbsp; it does me to &#8212; and i don&#8217;t know why.&nbsp; second, my service level changes concern me.&nbsp; i have nothing against cox as an organization, but others who have dealt with them and their services haven&#8217;t had good things to say.&nbsp; from the internet service standpoint, i&#8217;ve heard nothing but complaints: outages, blocked ports, no &#8220;home office&#8221; offering (must upgrade to higher priced business class&#8212;which is not really any more value), and crappy customer service/response times.&nbsp; i looked at current pricing and based on all my services, if cox were to take over today i&#8217;d be paying more money for less services and perceived quality.&nbsp; i&#8217;m not sure what their HDTV offerings are yet, but their site seems to list some offerings for my area &#8212; however friends have also questioned this service quality.</P>
<P>i hope the transition will take into consideration the customer.&nbsp; but often it doesn&#8217;t.&nbsp; we&#8217;ll have to wait and see.&nbsp; my father just started using qwest high speed and likes it &#8212; maybe i&#8217;ll have to give them another look&#8230;</P>
