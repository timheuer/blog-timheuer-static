<p>i just got a note from jamesb who is an <a title="AZGroups" href="http://www.azgroups.com" target="_blank">AZGroups</a>.com frequenter.  he reports that he got vista running on a vpc.  while some may have already done this, others have struggled still.  james pointed to a resource that helped him get it running using microsoft virtual pc (now free).</p> <p>he used: <a href="http://www.computerperformance.co.uk/vista/virtualpc2004.htm">vista on virtual pc</a></p> <p>he noted to me that it is running on 812MB RAM and took &gt;8GB of the drive -- oh yeah, and took about 14 hours to install...yikes.</p> <p>just a reminder that vista on vpc isn't a good experience at all and you aren't getting the full avalon...er, um...WPF experience.  it may be good for minor testing, but don't judge your opinion of features/perf on that environment.</p> <p>on a side note, the virtual cd-rom control panel that microsoft had been distributing (the tool that loads a driver letting you mount ISO images as drives) does *not* work on vista in my experiences.  argh.  the good news is that there is another free tool from slysoft called <a href="http://www.slysoft.com/en/virtual-clonedrive.html" rel="tag">virtual clone drive</a> that is free and works on vista...it works a little differently in that you have to determine how many virtual drives you want upfront (versus just adding/deleteing easily in vcdrom), but it is just a few extra clicks -- nothing you'd likely be doing a lot.</p> <p></p>
