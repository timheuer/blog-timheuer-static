<p>so at tech lunch wednesday here in phoenix, after lunch i sat with <a href="http://community.axosoft.com/blogs/hamids/default.aspx">hamid</a> and <a href="http://www.scottcate.com">scott</a> for a bit and we were brainstorming about a few things.  one of which was storage, then we got on the topic of amazon's s3 solution.  i had started to look at it before, but then never got the time to go back.  essentially amazon provides storage via a web service (there are no tools provided by them, just an api).  i said that i mainly use flickr for the storage and that i'd only use it if i could get a direct url to things, and that i'd have to have a plugin for live writer :-).</p> <p>well, when i got back i started to mess around a bit and got a working progress for my "s3 browser" live writer plugin.  you can browse your buckets and link to an item as a link or an image.  it is very rough right now, has some issues with UI threads, etc., but it works for me so far.</p> <p>you can take a look at the screenshot below -- and the link is directly to my s3 stored image!</p> <p><a href="http://s3.amazonaws.com/timheuer-img/sshot-29.png" atomicselection="true"><img height="353" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/timheuer-img/sshot-29.png" width="500" border="0" /></a></p> <div class="wlWriterSmartContent" id="0767317B-992E-4b12-91E0-4F059A8CECA8:19bed7fd-99c9-4c84-85ae-dfb515e5a8e4" contenteditable="false" style="padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-left: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; margin: 0px; padding-top: 0px"></div>
