<p>do you live in an area that has a lot of graffiti?  when you walk buy you say, 'dang, that looks really good, but i just wish it wasn't painted on in my neighborhood'?  maybe, maybe not.</p> <p>i saw a post about dome digital taggers.  using photography and time-lapse techniques, they are able to lay down some light (as in the luminous kind) graffiti.  check out the <a href="http://www.colourlovers.com/blog/2007/07/09/colored-in-dancing-lights/">painting in light post</a> as well as the <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/lichtfaktor/">flickr set</a> of other photos.  they use light sticks, etc. whatever works to get the final product.</p> <p><a title="Berlin Dom" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/9026201@N05/659464879/"><img alt="Berlin Dom" src="http://static.flickr.com/1012/659464879_d2b97e1c5d.jpg" border="0" /></a></p> <p>what an innovative way of using technology and mixing it with a different type of photography genre...i'm going to have to try it out.</p> <div class="wlWriterSmartContent" id="scid:0767317B-992E-4b12-91E0-4F059A8CECA8:dc4f68c9-2b08-49b4-bfc6-d78ee62ef4c3" style="padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-left: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; margin: 0px; padding-top: 0px"></div>
