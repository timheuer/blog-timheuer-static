<p>well, it has happened...the rumors are true.</p> <p><img src="http://www.blogsmithmedia.com/www.engadget.com/media/2007/01/dsc_0184.jpg" /> <br />(picture of <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2007/01/09/the-apple-iphone/">engadget.com</a>)</p> <p>seriously, the <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2007/01/09/the-apple-iphone/">pictures say a lot from the engadget post</a>.  i am really amazed/impressed.  a few things that are interesting about it is that it doesn't support 3G and the touch screen.  I've had a few full touch-screen devices before and didn't like them.  my greasy face 'yucked' the screen up during calls, etc.</p> <p>but c'mon...OSX on a device with iPod, with full browser, etc., etc.  this seems to be a well done v1 product.  this is what makes them win in these types of areas.  they took the free research and innovated.  when i say free research, i mean the feedback from all other devices from all the other vendors, etc.  as a contrast to zune for example -- i keep hearing zune is a 'good v1 device' -- what!?  it could've been better -- we had 5 years of free research with ipods and didn't take advantage of that in my opinion.  apple iphone seems to have taken advantage of (sans touch screen).</p> <div class="wlWriterSmartContent" id="0767317B-992E-4b12-91E0-4F059A8CECA8:22ad9de8-df45-4a8b-b6de-43411f1161c4" contenteditable="false" style="padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-left: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; margin: 0px; padding-top: 0px"></div>
