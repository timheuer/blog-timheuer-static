<p>as i thought i learned when <A href="http://timheuer.com/blog/archive/2006/01/23/7135.aspx">i fought the law</a>, that we are all equal in the eyes and letter of the law.&nbsp; some events today made me quickly realize my naivity.</p>
<p>let&rsquo;s start with my commute.&nbsp; i live pretty far away from our office here in phoenix&hellip;and this morning my commute in was 1 hour, 23 minutes.&nbsp; now i realize in some areas this is nothing, but in phoenix, i&rsquo;m going to go ahead and call that a long time.&nbsp; during my commute, there is a carpool/hov lane the whole way in (must have 2 people in the car to use from 6&ndash;9 AM, 3&ndash;7PM).&nbsp; i can tell you that throughout the times i&rsquo;ve been making this commute, that carpool lane is empty relative to the other three lanes on the freeway &mdash; don&rsquo;t get me started on the whole friggin carpool lane thing&hellip;it&rsquo;s worthless (not that i&rsquo;m against the environment, clean air, etc. &mdash; but let&rsquo;s be real, we&rsquo;d all be happier if we could use that lane).</p>
<p>okay, the carpool lane is what caught my eye this morning&hellip;as a police car (not a highway dps vehicle, but a normal city of phoenix vehicle) was in the carpool lane (only one officer in the car) and during the areas of tempe, az (not phoenix).&nbsp; he was not speeding.&nbsp; he was not passing cars.&nbsp; there were no accidents.&nbsp; there were no emergency lights on his vehicle.&nbsp; you get my point &mdash; he wasn&rsquo;t involved in any seemingly emergency that would normally cause emergency vehicles to use that lane.&nbsp; he knew that he wouldn&rsquo;t get pulled over.&nbsp; so where do we draw the line on something so minor?&nbsp; should *any* law enforcement be able to do this?&nbsp; i&rsquo;m sure he had places to be, but so did i.&nbsp; now, my educational background is law and most of my friends are in law enforcement.&nbsp; they enjoy telling me stories of how they get pulled over frequently for speeding, show their badge and move on.&nbsp; is that right?&nbsp; why are law enforcement officials above the law even in the most minor incidents?</p>
<p>this is a minor infraction, i agree, and mostly an annoyance.&nbsp; but still a violation of the law nonetheless&hellip;after all, i was taking a shortcut and was cited&hellip;why shouldn&rsquo;t a law enforcement official (on or off duty), not involved in any police situation, be allowed a free pass?</p>
<p>okay, now on to something more serious.&nbsp; imagine you were hunting with friends and an accident occurred.&nbsp; you go to the hospital and tell them your friend was shot.&nbsp; don&rsquo;t you think that law enforcement would immediately be called (regardless of the story you tell) just to investigate/report?&nbsp; i guarantee it (at least in the US).&nbsp; it&rsquo;s the law to report these things.&nbsp; </p>
<p>as equal is the importance of health officials to report potential child abuse.&nbsp; if a child (again, in the US) comes into an emergency center badly bruised, etc. it is likely that someone will be called in to ensure no parental neglect/abuse is taking place.&nbsp; while uncomfortable, it might be the right thing to do.</p>
<p>so, here&rsquo;s my point (and i&rsquo;m sure you&rsquo;ve heard it recently).&nbsp; our vice president, dick cheney, had such a hunting accident recently.&nbsp; reading in the news today, we found that <a href="http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/11340558/">cheney was cited</a> (with a warning&mdash;along with his buddies) for a permit violation.&nbsp; i&rsquo;m glad he was cited &mdash; it appears a new law went into effect but not many people knew about it, hence the warning.&nbsp; but on to the disturbing part:</p>
<blockquote>Secret Service spokesman Eric Zahren said that about an hour after Cheney shot Whittington, the head of the Secret Service&rsquo;s local office called the Kenedy County sheriff to report the accident. &ldquo;They made arrangements at the sheriff&rsquo;s request to have deputies come out and interview the vice president the following morning at 8 a.m. and that indeed did happen,&rdquo; Zahren said.</blockquote>
<p>&nbsp;to me, this is unacceptable.&nbsp; why didn&rsquo;t the hospital staff report it?&nbsp; if it was an accident, why such the hush?&nbsp; also, interviewed THE NEXT MORNING?!?!?!?!&nbsp; i think only oj simpson and dick cheney have been afforded this privilage of deciding when they can be questioned in accidents.</p>
<p>i&rsquo;m sure it was an accident, i have no doubt.&nbsp; i love conspiracies, but i don&rsquo;t think this is one.&nbsp; i just don&rsquo;t get why there is such preferential treatment.&nbsp; i loved how one reported said it in a news conference:</p>
<blockquote>"Let's just be clear here," Gregory said. "The vice president of the United States accidentally shoots a man, and he feels that it's appropriate for a ranch owner who witnessed this to tell the local Corpus Christi newspaper and not the White House press corps at large or notify the public in a national way?"</blockquote>
<p>anyhow, it pissed me off.&nbsp; again, if anyone else in the common world had this happen, we wouldn&rsquo;t be able to wait to be questioned on our own time, regardless of if it was an accident.</p>
<p>argh&hellip;politicians.</p>
