<p>I’ve just completed my upgrade to my site of the <strong>official 2.0 release of </strong><a href="http://www.subtextproject.com/"><strong>Subtext</strong></a>, the Open Source blogging engine that I use to manage content on this site.  You can read the full announcement from grand <a href="http://haacked.com/archive/2008/08/10/subtext-2.0-released.aspx">poohba Haack himself here</a>.</p>  <p><img style="margin: 0px 10px 0px 0px" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com/timheuer-img/subtextwlw.png" align="left" /> I’m loving this release because of the improvements made but also a little selfishly because the <a href="http://timheuer.com/blog/archive/2008/06/25/first-contribution-made-to-open-source-subtext.aspx">modifications I’ve made to my own fork</a> I’ve been using have made it into this release!  These modifications really make this the best platform for me when using <a href="http://writer.live.com">Live Writer</a>.  This may not make a difference to a lot of you, but it really is exciting for me to see my check-ins make it into the release and to not have to use my custom assemblies anymore!</p>  <p>Thanks to the Subtext team for supporting my contributions and I look forward to seeing how I can participate in v3!  <strong><a href="https://sourceforge.net/project/showfiles.php?group_id=137896">Get your Subtext 2.0 upgrade today</a></strong>!</p>  <div class="wlWriterSmartContent" id="scid:0767317B-992E-4b12-91E0-4F059A8CECA8:5a4d1dbb-dd3b-40aa-a0b3-f3f1b196c1f0" style="padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-left: 0px; float: none; padding-bottom: 0px; margin: 0px; padding-top: 0px"></div>
