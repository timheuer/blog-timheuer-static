<p>today is the start of the first day of the second microsoft technology summit (mts06).</p>
<p>what is mts?&nbsp; well it&rsquo;s an opportunity we (msft) take to invite the more broader audiences outside of microsoft (read: generally the &lsquo;anything but microsoft&rsquo; crowd) to redmond to have a 2&ndash;day conversation with key technologists within microsoft.</p>
<p>is the goal to convert the attendees?&nbsp; no.&nbsp; although admittedly that would be a great byproduct for us&hellip;but NOT the goal at all.&nbsp; the goal is to help change perception of what we are doing, and to mainly listen.&nbsp; we are listening to the feedback from this crowd.&nbsp; will it immediately impact change in the short term for products that are in the path of completion?&nbsp; probably not to be honest&hellip;but it *will* influence. </p>
<p>the hosts for this event are the technologist elite: don box, chris anderson, anders hejlsberg, rick rashid, jim gray, scott guthrie, bill hilf, etc, etc. &mdash; these are the people that are at the right levels in the right areas to hear the feedback.</p>
<p>i&rsquo;ll try to report as much as i can from the event &mdash; it should be interesting.&nbsp; to protect the privacy of the attendees, i won&rsquo;t iterate through them, but know that the java, linux, php elite are here.&nbsp; the influencers in apache are here.&nbsp; community leaders in open source movements are here.&nbsp; if they aren&rsquo;t&hellip;they were likely invited and had some conflicts.&nbsp; a very small crowd (52 from around the world).</p>
