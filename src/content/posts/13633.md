<p>i've been looking at options on feedburner and saw something called a 'photo splicer' that you can add to your feed.  basically your provide them your user name of a photo service and they'll add that feed into your current feedburner feed -- a feed merge if you will.</p> <p>cool, i thought i'd try it out (i immediately took it down because for me it just didn't make sense).  the first thing i noticed was something akin to seth godin's new site <a href="http://www.thisisbroken.com">www.thisisbroken.com</a>.  here was one of the configuration options:</p> <p><img alt="FB_PhotoSplicer" src="http://static.flickr.com/83/267523721_3cbe0697a2.jpg" border="0" /></p> <p>note the text box label: "Flickr ID or Screen Name" -- it says "or Screen Name" right?  i'm not just seeing things?  okay, so i try the screen name...why?  because that is what every flickr user immediately knows, duh.  error.  "XXXXXX" is not a valid flickr id.  yeah, i knew that...your label said "or" so i'm trying the "or."  okay, clearly it wasn't working.  now, i was smart enough to read the fine print that is there, but heck it still said "or" on the label.  now take a moment and read the fine print.  okay, quick, what's your flickr id?  exactly.</p> <p>feedburner is basically saying: "okay, go here, do this, look at the url, and get this random string of characters" -- yeah, unintuitive.  by the way feedburner...my flickr photostream doesn't have that :-) -- guess what it has?  my screen name.  argh.  <strong>and by the way, the flickr api's allow you to take a screen name and interpret the id...so feedburner...don't make me work so hard</strong>!</p> <div class="wlWriterSmartContent" id="0767317B-992E-4b12-91E0-4F059A8CECA8:65a445bd-fcb9-4b4b-9d8d-da6ce0fdd934" contenteditable="false" style="padding-right: 0px; display: inline; padding-left: 0px; padding-bottom: 0px; margin: 0px; padding-top: 0px"></div>
