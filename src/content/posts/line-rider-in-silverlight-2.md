<p><a href="http://www.linerider.com"><img title="Bosch" style="MARGIN: 0px 10px 0px 0px" alt="Bosch" align="left" border="0" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com:80/timheuer-img/bosch-225.png" /></a>One of probably the coolest casual ‘games’ I’ve ever seen is <a href="http://www.linerider.com"><strong>Line Rider</strong></a>.  It’s a fun game, but really gets exciting when you have an artist behind it.  Basically it’s a blank canvas for you to draw a route for the main character to hop on his sled and ride.  Sound lame?  Not when you see what some have come up with.  For example, here’s a very <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bcu8ZdJ2dQo">popular ‘ride’ on YouTube</a>.</p>
<p>Well, the Line Rider team has recently modified their beta version and implemented in <a href="http://silverlight.net"><strong>Silverlight</strong></a> 2!  They’ve also added some social networking integration using Live Messenger and the ability to send your rides to your contacts, etc.  The team was a group of developers and designers from the Line Rider team from <strong><a href="http://www.inxile-entertainment.com/">InXile Entertainment</a></strong> as well as the crew from <strong><a href="http://www.cynergysystems.com/">Cynergy Systems</a></strong>.  The Line Rider game was also used in a creative McDonald’s television campaign as well – McDonald’s gettin’ all involved with the interwebs…cool.</p>
<p>In addition to the Silverlight version, InXile has announced console versions for <strong>XBOX 360</strong>, <strong>Wii</strong>, and <strong>PS3</strong>.  I think that will definitely a must get console game for myself and for the kids.  This Silverlight version really demonstrates some great things in Silverlight 2 and shows that the platform is a great one for casual games and how creative you can be with what version 2 has to offer.</p>
<p>Congratulations to the Line Rider teams!</p>
