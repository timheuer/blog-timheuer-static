After having a conversation with <a href="http://blogs.msdn.com/bgold">Brian Goldfarb</a> (ASPNET Product Manager), I have better insight into these jobs below.
<br><br>
This is a part of a study to research developer productivity.
<br><br>
<b>What is it?</b> A contract job &#8211; it is not a long-term, full-time job.
<b>How much do I get paid?</b> I won&rsquo;t quote the terms here, but if you are interested, you should apply.  I will say it probably averages around $50/hr.
<b>That&rsquo;s it?</b> Yeah, but they pay for all T&E: travel, a place to stay (this is not local to any of you), food, etc.
<b>How long is it for?</b> They tell me about a week.
<b>What else do I get out of it?</b> The chance to be a part of a really cool study, network with MS product team folks, free training, massive networking with others, and why not, a resume builder!
<br><br>
Seriously, this sounded like a really cool opportunity.  It is essentially a productivity study.  You will be provided with a completed spec and asked to build the application.  The measurements will be based on completion and there will be several teams doing it.  It really sounded like fun&#8230;and you get paid for it.
