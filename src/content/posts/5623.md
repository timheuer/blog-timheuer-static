<p>well, we just finished yesterday with the <a href="http://www.dotnetrocks.com/roadtrip">.net rocks roadtrip</a> visiting phoenix.&nbsp; it was great to host these guys and i appreciated <a href="http://weblogs.asp.net/palermo4">palermo4</a> setting it all up for our community.</p>
<p>after the show, which was well attended and a different audience than our normal phoenix crowd, <a href="http://ww.franklins.net/">carl </a>and <a href="http://www.campbellassociates.ca/blog/">richard </a>invited us into the &ldquo;<a href="http://www.dotnetrocks.com/roadtrip/photos/RT4_TheBoat.jpg">boat</a>&rdquo; to record a show.</p>
<p>a few of the smart guys from <a href="http://www.terralever.com/">terralever </a>where there to chat about integrating asp.net web services and applications into flash applications and some of the challenges faced with that implementation.&nbsp; We also had <a href="http://timheuer.com/blog/archive/2005/11/02/5591.aspx">noah</a>&nbsp;in there who was trying to get richard&rsquo;s job, which was pretty funny &mdash; he had a good pitch though.&nbsp; one of our new visual basic mvp&rsquo;s, steele price, also stopped by to chat along with a few other pretty smart devs (or at least that&rsquo;s what they tell me) ;-).</p>
<p>in between the recording of interviews, i had a chance to chat with richard/carl about random stuff and podcasting.&nbsp; the conversation was certainly geeky at times, but i learned a lot about how they make their show the best.&nbsp; these guys really are professionals and take this seriously.&nbsp; from noise cancelling, to edit points, to subtle queues, they really have it down.&nbsp; truly the original podcast.&nbsp; we chatted about podcasting and how i felt everyone is a podcaster now, diluting the real value of the meaning of podcast feeds (i.e., information to you).&nbsp; i think there are some out there just to be out there &mdash; but .net rocks is really a professional show.&nbsp; the beauty apparently is (not only the content of course) in the post-editing to make the show the quality, in both content and audio, and jeff is the man for their show.&nbsp; he does a great job with the voodoo that he doo.&nbsp; i didn&rsquo;t get a chance to meet jeff, but i have a better appreciation for the time he spends editing.</p>
<p>all-in-all, it was&nbsp;a great experience, and i really felt richard/carl are great, sincere, down-to-earth guys&hellip;even if richard is canadian ;-) &mdash; but i had a great time geeking out (still trying to explain it to my wife) till the wee hours of the evening&hellip;thanks guys! oh yeah, and the clementine renditions were pretty good too!</p>
