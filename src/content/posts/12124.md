<p>i&rsquo;ve been using gmail as a shell to my mail.&nbsp; i don&rsquo;t really *use* gmail, but i like their interface and it&rsquo;s easy to use, so i guess i do use it :-)</p>
<p>anyhow, i started using it once they enabled the &ldquo;from&rdquo; address customization, where i could set the from address to one of my liking.</p>
<p>until recently when i&rsquo;ve realized it isn&rsquo;t exactly doing what i thought/want.&nbsp; when i get a message in my outlook i see <a href="mailto:&ldquo;me@gmail.com">&ldquo;me@gmail.com</a> on behalf of <a href="mailto:me@myrealemail.com">me@myrealemail.com</a>&rdquo; (obviously changed the email addresses to protect spam here) which isn&rsquo;t exactly the desired outcome.</p>
<p>a quick review of the headers shows:</p>
<p>From: <a href="mailto:me@myrealemail.com">me@myrealemail.com</a><br />Sender: <a href="mailto:me@gmail.com">me@gmail.com</a></p>
<p>so if gmail offers the feature, why do they need to alter the sender header?&nbsp; why not make the from really who i want it to be from?&nbsp; after all, isn&rsquo;t that what they are selling as the feature?</p>
<p>argh.</p>
