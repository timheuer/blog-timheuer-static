<p>anyone who knows my woes with fulton homes knows i hate terrible customer service &mdash; even if things are my fault, there&rsquo;s no room for customer service.</p>
<p>let me share some tag lines with you:</p>
<ul>
<li>Better Service&hellip;Get the ADVANTAGE!</li>
<li>The Service YOU Deserve</li></ul>
<p>now, my check engine light came on in my car.&nbsp; it&rsquo;s out of warranty, but in prestine condition (that is one good thing about snowbirds ;-)).&nbsp; so i have one of those diagnostic OBD tools and it tells me some things are wrong with the secondary air injection.&nbsp; i know computers.&nbsp; i don&rsquo;t know engines.&nbsp; quite frankly, the only reason i have one of those OBD tools is because the geek in me thought it would be cool to diagnose my own problems [and then go to someone else anyway to diagnose/repair].</p>
<p>i digress&hellip;</p>
<p>well, i didn&rsquo;t really feel like paying the dealer labor fee (US $170/hr), so i thought i&rsquo;d find a non-dealer shop.&nbsp; everytime i look cheaper i always remind myself &lsquo;you get what you pay for&rsquo; but i always try anyway.&nbsp; i found two service centers that would service my car &mdash; their slogans above.&nbsp; i called both.&nbsp; i assure you neither lived up to their slogan.&nbsp; i implore you &mdash; if you use a tagline&hellip;at least have your front-line people assume they live up to it!&nbsp; based on the 5 minute phone call and the jackasses at each place telling me it would take 4 hours to just *tell* me what was wrong (note: my OBD tool told me in &lt; 3 minutes), i don&rsquo;t think i&rsquo;m going to get &lsquo;the service I deserve&rsquo; if i go to them.</p>
<p>there&rsquo;s something to be said about higher prices &mdash; the 170/hr place (i&rsquo;ve been there before) gives me a free loaner car, will diagnose immediately and usually fits it in the same day to fix.&nbsp; do i end up paying more?&nbsp; questionable.&nbsp; if i saved headaches and time, probably not.</p>
