<p><a href="http://www.silverlightshow.net/SilverlightContestWriteAndWin.aspx"><img style="border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; margin: 0px 10px 0px 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px" border="0" align="left" src="http://storage.timheuer.com/silverlight_contest.gif" /></a>Got a great concept about <a href="http://silverlight.net">Silverlight</a> use, an application, utility, or just some knowledge you want to share but never had the place to put it?  The team at <a href="http://silverlightshow.net">SilverlightShow</a> have been having a contest they call <em>Silverlight: Write and Win!</em> and you can get some great Silverlight-related prizes such as Telerik and ComponentOne control licenses for Silverlight and WPF applications.</p>  <p>You have to simply write an article about your concept (application, control, whatever) related to Silverlight 2 and provide the code (under an <a href="http://www.opensource.org/licenses/alphabetical">OSI</a> approved license) and you’ll be entered – submit as many times as you want.  The “judges” are a mix of community members and Microsoft guys that will be reviewing the content and code.</p>  <p>So what are you waiting for, enter the <em><a href="http://www.silverlightshow.net/SilverlightContestWriteAndWin.aspx">Silverlight: Write and Win!</a></em> contest…you have less than 30 days left!</p>  <div style="padding-bottom: 0px; margin: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; float: none; padding-top: 0px" id="scid:0767317B-992E-4b12-91E0-4F059A8CECA8:51f0e4f3-cd64-4d76-87e3-cccbe11ad81d" class="wlWriterEditableSmartContent"></div>
