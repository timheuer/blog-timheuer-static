<p>Remember <a href="http://labs.live.com/photosynth/">Photosynth</a>?  Remember when you first saw it and your initial smile came across you in that ‘this is cool’ kind of feeling?</p>
<p>Multiply that.</p>
<p>A team at the University of Washington in conjunction with Microsoft Research presented “Finding Paths Through the World’s Photos” at SIGGRAPH2008.  I haven’t read the paper yet, but the video speaks for itself in the advancements of photo recognition and path interpolation to me:</p>
<p><embed src="http://www.youtube.com/v/gLLzV5qeKyk&amp;color1=11645361&amp;color2=13619151&amp;hl=en&amp;fs=1" width="425" height="344" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" wmode="transparent" allowfullscreen="true" /></p>
<p>There is some really similar Photosynth and DeepZoom stuff happening here, but a lot more as well.  You can <a href="http://phototour.cs.washington.edu/findingpaths/">visit their site to read more</a> and also download some code from some of the parts of the system.</p>
<p><strong>UPDATE: And when you are done watching this, check out </strong><a href="http://research.microsoft.com/unwrap/"><strong>Unwrap </strong></a><strong>"editing video is now as easy as editing a single image." -- Um freaky.</strong></p>
