<p>We’ve just released an update to our <a href="http://silverlight.net/"><strong>Silverlight</strong></a><strong> Enterprise Deployment </strong>guidance/whitepaper.  While a lot of the information from the first version of the guidance (targeted at Silverlight 1.0), we made some minor adjustments outlining the specifics for the updated Silverlight installer and documented some of the command-switches and their behaviors.  </p>  <p>The guidance covers:</p>  <ul>   <li>Using Windows Software Update Services (WSUS)</li>    <li>Group policy</li>    <li>Microsoft System Center Configuration Manager (SCCM)</li>    <li>Manual install options</li>    <li>Maintaining and detecting version</li> </ul>  <p>It’s a great guidance document to keep in your toolkit if you are in charge of deployment in your organization or want a better way to manage mass deployments of Silverlight across the organization.  You can download the <a href="http://silverlight.net/learn/whitepapers.aspx"><strong>Silverlight Enterprise Deployment</strong></a> whitepaper from the <a href="http://silverlight.net/">Silverlight community site</a>.</p>  <div style="padding-bottom: 0px; margin: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; float: none; padding-top: 0px" id="scid:0767317B-992E-4b12-91E0-4F059A8CECA8:95bf183f-73c4-4643-82be-851394961965" class="wlWriterEditableSmartContent"></div>
