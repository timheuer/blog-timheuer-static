<p>i missed the keynote due to some meetings, but had a chance to have breakfast with <a href="http://blog.joshuaeichorn.com/">josh</a>, who is the author of the PHP ajax library, <a href="http://pear.php.net/package/HTML_AJAX/">HTML_AJAX</a>.&nbsp; i was glad to hear some positive experiences josh is having at the conference.</p>
<p>right now i&rsquo;m in the &lsquo;building better user experiences&rdquo; for web applications, with rick spencer and brad abrams.&nbsp; first, they came out and were very &ldquo;fluid&rdquo; &mdash; great energy, non-technical &ldquo;here is my powerpoint bullets, don&rsquo;t ask any questions&rdquo; feel to it.&nbsp; they immediately engaged the audience on some questions to setup their discussion.</p>
<p>rick is talking now &mdash; not behind the podium &mdash; i like that.</p>
<p>rick referred to two pices of information, usable <a href="http://ajaxpatterns.org/">ajax patterns</a>&nbsp;by&nbsp;<a href="http://mahemoff.com/">michael mahemoff</a> and <a href="http://www.baekdal.com/articles/Usability/usable-XMLHttpRequest/">usability guidelines</a>&nbsp;by&nbsp;<a href="http://www.baekdal.com/">thomas beakdal</a></p>
<p>lessons learned &ndash; rick talked about a few principles of interactive development &mdash; i couldn&rsquo;t capture them all, but here are some notes&hellip;</p>
<p>1) instant feedback &ndash; providing instant feedback on what is happening even if it isn&rsquo;t happening</p>
<p>2) predict &ndash; showing the map dragging &mdash; bringing around the surrounding images shows the predictibility of what the user will do next; other sample is email address (yahoo), another sample is flickr slideshow &mdash; getting next image as the natural selection is the next image.</p>
<p>3) preserve &ndash; don&rsquo;t forget about what the user has done</p>
<p>4) share &ndash; making sure when the user finds something of value it is important to enable the ability to share. demonstrating the permalink f a map view on virtual earth (suggestion for virtual earth &mdash; create shrinkster-ish URLs for your site)</p>
<p>overall this wasn&rsquo;t any new information that i learned personally, but they did show some more atlas behaviors that i&rsquo;d like to get my hands on&hellip;everyone keeps showing them, but nowhere to download just yet. (wish they&rsquo;d adopt the &lsquo;share&rsquo; principle here ;-)).</p>
<p>rick and brad took the time for questions (thanks!!!!!)&nbsp; rick mentioned that i threw him a fast ball when i inquired about the MILF factor.&nbsp; no, not that one&hellip;the:</p>
<p>Mother<br />In<br />Law<br />Factor</p>
<p>and how it applies to bookmarking ajax-ish sites (where state has changed multiple times but the url hasn&rsquo;t) and the back button issues.</p>
