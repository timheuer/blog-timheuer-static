<p><a href="http://www.silverlightshow.net/">SilverlightShow.net</a> is one of those great community sites providing information, tutorials and general developer information around <a href="http://www.silverlight.net">Silverlight</a>.  They’ve done a great job providing a spectrum of beginner to advanced topics on Silverlight development.  </p>  <p>At MIX10 I was talking with one of the brains behind the site (and Silverlight MVP), <a href="http://emil.silverlightshow.net/">Emil Stoychev</a>, one one of the ways they had been displaying book recommendations on their site.  It was a bookshelf view that I thought was cool and I wanted to use it for my own book recommendation list on my blog.  </p>  <blockquote>   <p>NOTE: It was funny in talking with Emil because they had this bookshelf control for a while and then the iBooks app for iPhone/iPad came out and looked really familiar.  Of course, the user experience isn’t something entirely new for displaying this type of information, but it was cool to see consistency.</p> </blockquote>  <p>I asked Emil if I could use the control and showcase some of the work they do on that site.  They carved off a little area where I could manage my books and here it is:</p>  <p><img style="display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto" title="SilverlightShow bookshelf" alt="SilverlightShow bookshelf" src="http://s3.amazonaws.com:80/storage2.timheuer.com/bookshelf.png" /></p>  <p>You can see it running live and interact with it on my <strong><a href="http://timheuer.com/blog/articles/silverlight-book-recommendations.aspx">Silverlight Book Recommendations</a></strong> link here on this site.  I think it is a lot better than just a standard list of links.  Now I just need to fill up the shelves so they don’t look so empty!</p>  <p>Thanks Emil and SilverlightShow!  And to answer in advance, I’m not sure if they plan on releasing this as a sample control or what…that is something you’ll have to head over to their site and ask them (and check out their material as well).   </p><div style="padding-bottom: 0px;
