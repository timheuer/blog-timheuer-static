<p>this weekend i decided to go shopping with my wife&hellip;well, okay, she shopped, i waited in chairs most of the time outside of dressing rooms.</p>
<p>during one store visit, there was a t-mobile store next to it, so i decided to just wander and check out the devices (which i already knew which ones they had).</p>
<p>the conversation went something like this:</p>
<p><strong>employee:</strong> how can i help you tonight?<br /><strong>me:</strong> ah, just killing time while my wife is shopping&hellip;<br /><strong>employee: </strong>i see, so are you already with t-mobile?<br /><strong>me:</strong> actually, i just switched<br /><strong>employee:</strong> really, may i ask why?<br /><strong>me:</strong> sure, i switched to verizon for the faster data speeds.<br /><strong>employee:</strong> oh, well, you know they aren&rsquo;t that much faster<br /><strong>me:</strong> dude, step off, i&rsquo;m a techno-dweeb and trust me, you don&rsquo;t want to engage in the conversation you are about to enter.&nbsp; i&rsquo;ll disorient you with a bunch of acronyms like CDMA, GSM, TDMA, EVDO and spout off frequencies of the spectrum you haven&rsquo;t even considered for&hellip;your head will start spinning you&rsquo;ll wish you never asked.</p>
<p>okay, i really didn&rsquo;t say that, but did tell him he was dead wrong and that my personal experience was that verizon was about 7x faster than the EDGE service i had w/t-mobile.&nbsp; he insisted t-mobile was faster and then i finally had to shut him down a bit rudely and let him know that he really wasn&rsquo;t equipped to make that statement.</p>
<p>it got me thinking later that night (actually about 5 minutes after that) and while on my treo 700w, i went to a mobile speed test site (DSL Reports MSpeed).&nbsp; my results were actually pretty interesting.&nbsp; you first choose your &lsquo;data chunk&rsquo; size you want to test with: 5K, 20K, 100K, 200K, 600K, 1MB.&nbsp; on all but the 600K i got this message (screen caps from my actual device):</p>
<p><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/timheuer/133030959/"><img alt="toofast200k" src="http://static.flickr.com/49/133030959_2186fd8b1b_m.jpg" border="0" /></a></p>
<p>so when i tried it with the 600K size file:</p>

<p>and with the 1MB file:</p>

<p>pretty dang good&hellip; now i don&rsquo;t have my t-mobile device anymore with me, but i can tell you that the fastest speed (and yes, on EDGE) that i ever received was 173kbit/sec.&nbsp; so my 7x comment was specific to me.&nbsp; i realize that there are definitely different considerations and &lsquo;your speeds may vary&rsquo; does apply, which led me to click on dslreports&rsquo; &lsquo;fastest speeds&rsquo; link to show the summaries by sample:</p>
<p><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/timheuer/133030949/"><img alt="fastest" src="http://static.flickr.com/46/133030949_c59d2fd298_m.jpg" border="0" /></a></p>
<p>notice that sprint (spcsdns.net) and verizon (myvzw.com) are the two fastest.&nbsp; and notice where t-mobile (tmodns.net) is?&nbsp; almost 3x difference.&nbsp; And they have about the same sampling size.&nbsp; so my comment was pretty accurate.&nbsp; the report site also had a link of &lsquo;fastest today&rsquo; which i thought had intriguing results:</p>
<p><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/timheuer/133030947/"><img alt="fastesttoday" src="http://static.flickr.com/54/133030947_65c8b523de.jpg" border="0" /></a></p>
<p>noticing that t-mobile had posted some pretty good speeds (also notice the broswers there).&nbsp; now i&rsquo;m one to admit when i&rsquo;m wrong and at first glance the &lsquo;fastest today&rsquo; speeds show t-mobile faster (albeit not by much) than sprint/verizon (both using EVDO technology).&nbsp; however, when you apply the sampling rate to get averages, i think it rains supreme that t-mobile (EDGE) is still a bit slower.</p>
