<p>so, i&rsquo;ve officially not checked my newsgator subscription for 30+ days.&nbsp; i&rsquo;ve exclusively been using <a href="http://www.squeet.com/">Squeet</a>.&nbsp; why?&nbsp; well first, when presented with a challenge, i like to take it.&nbsp; <a href="http://blogs.axosoft.com/hamids">hamid</a> told me to use it for a while and see if it works better for my needs over newsgator.&nbsp; for me, it has.&nbsp; (hopefully he&rsquo;s equally taken my challenge to use search.msn.com over google for 30 days.)&nbsp; second, i like it.&nbsp; rss in my email, and no software &mdash; i can use my OWA to read it as well.&nbsp; i define the schedule on a feed-by-feed basis.&nbsp; i&rsquo;m up to 270 feeds now and i like the organization i get with squeet.</p>
<p>anyhow, one thing i didn&rsquo;t like was finding a feed, was copying, going to squeet, entering the url, etc.</p>
<p>so i remembered how bloglines did it, and i&rsquo;m copying their idea.</p>
<p>here, right click on <a href="javascript:location.href='http://www.squeet.com/?FeedUrl='+location.href">THIS LINK</a> and choose to add to favorites (IE &mdash; FireFox users can drag the link to the bookmarks toolbar).&nbsp; Now you&rsquo;ll have a favorite where you can (i suggest naming it &lsquo;Squeet This&rsquo;) Squeet your current link you are on.&nbsp; and because their service looks for discovery info as well, you may not even have to hunt for the feed!</p>
<p>hope this helps!</p>
<p>NOTE: this little feature doesn&rsquo;t work with pure xml docs</p>
